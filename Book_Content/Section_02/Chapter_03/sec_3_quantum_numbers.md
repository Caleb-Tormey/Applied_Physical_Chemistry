## Quantum Numbers

The solutions to the Schr√∂dinger equation for atoms naturally produce four quantum numbers: $n$, $l$, $m_l$, and $m_s$. These numbers are the "address" of an electron and explain the structure of the Periodic Table.

1.  **Principal Quantum Number ($n$):** $n = 1, 2, 3, \ldots$. This determines the **energy** and size of the orbital. In Hydrogen, all orbitals with the same $n$ are **degenerate** (have the same energy).
2.  **Angular Momentum ($l$):** $l = 0, 1, \dots, (n-1)$. This determines the **shape** ($s, p, d, f$).
3.  **Magnetic Quantum Number ($m_l$):** $m_l = -l, \dots, +l$. This determines the **orientation** in space.
4.  **Spin ($m_s$):** $m_s = \pm 1/2$. An intrinsic property of the electron.
