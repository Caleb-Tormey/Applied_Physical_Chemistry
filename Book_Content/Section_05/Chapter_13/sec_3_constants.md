## 13.3 Dissecting the Constants

The Michaelis-Menten equation gives us three crucial parameters that define an enzyme's identity. 

* **$V_{max}$ (Maximum Velocity):** This is the physical speed limit of the enzyme. It occurs when the substrate concentration is so incredibly high that every single enzyme active site is saturated ($[ES]$ equals the total enzyme concentration). At this point, adding more substrate does absolutely nothing. The reaction is temporarily zero-order.
* **$K_m$ (The Michaelis Constant):** Mathematically, $K_m$ is defined as the exact substrate concentration required to reach $\frac{1}{2} V_{max}$. Qualitatively, $K_m$ is a measure of **binding affinity**. A small $K_m$ means the enzyme reaches half-max speed with barely any substrate present, meaning it binds the substrate incredibly tightly. A large $K_m$ indicates weak, loose binding. 
* **$k_{cat}$ (Turnover Number):** If $V_{max}$ depends on how much total enzyme you put in the beaker, $k_{cat}$ is the intrinsic speed of a *single* enzyme molecule. It is the number of substrate molecules one enzyme can convert to product per second. 
    $$V_{max} = k_{cat}[E]_{total}$$
